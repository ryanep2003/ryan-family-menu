## Overall Impression

The app has a credible, warm product identity and meaningful household workflows rather than a generic dashboard shell. The biggest opportunity is not another visual restyle: it is making the fastest household actions obvious and equally available in English and Spanish, especially Add recipe, Week planning, and recipe cooking actions while translation is pending.

## What's Working

- The Today view gives the product a clear emotional and functional anchor with the Tonight meal band and Cook this action at `index.html:87`.
- Progressive disclosure is generally disciplined: calendar, grocery tools, inventory management, recipe actions, and recipe details are not all exposed at once.
- Localization is broad across shell labels, placeholders, status messages, ARIA labels, categories, and recipe content pathways in `translations.js`.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Add recipe is not a first-class destination

**Evidence:** The main navigation has four buttons at `index.html:44-49`, while Add is a separate view at `index.html:459-475`. `setView("add")` marks Recipes active at `app.js:938-944`.

**Why it matters:** Adding a recipe is a core caretaker workflow, but the navigation tells users that Recipes is the destination even when they are in a different view. This is a recognition and orientation problem, especially for first-time or low-technical-comfort users.

**Fix:** Either make Add a persistent fifth destination, or keep four tabs but add a persistent, clearly primary Add action in the shell and make the active state represent the actual Add view. Do not silently overload Recipes as the active destination.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify`

### [P1] Week planning is not glanceable on mobile

**Evidence:** At the mobile breakpoint, seven day summaries remain `min-width: 148px` in a horizontal scroller at `styles.css:2902-2909`. The editor is below the strip.

**Why it matters:** A caretaker in a store or kitchen must swipe to inspect the week and then move vertically to edit a day. That increases orientation cost and makes the weekly plan harder to compare at a glance.

**Fix:** Preserve the horizontal interaction only if it is proven useful, but add a compact current-week overview or a selected-day jump that keeps the editor adjacent to the selected card. Validate with 320px and 390px widths before changing desktop behavior.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable adapt`

### [P1] Pending Spanish recipe content can block cooking actions

**Evidence:** `recipe-library-ui.js:80-117` renders placeholders when localized name, ingredients, steps, or warning content is absent and disables Add ingredients to groceries and Mark cooked until `contentReady`. Backfill depends on the network function at `app.js:1114-1135`.

**Why it matters:** A Spanish-first household can see the shell translated but still be unable to use an already-known recipe while translation is pending or fails. The product becomes less functional exactly when the user toggles languages.

**Fix:** Keep the primary actions usable with an explicit source-language fallback, label the fallback clearly, cache successful translations locally, and make translation retry secondary rather than a gate for cooking or grocery actions.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

### [P2] Frequent actions are inconsistently hidden

**Evidence:** Grocery actions are inside `List tools` at `index.html:220-227`; inventory creation is inside `Manage` and nested details at `index.html:251-269`; recipe edit/favorite/cook actions are inside `More actions` at `index.html:356-366`.

**Why it matters:** Progressive disclosure reduces clutter, but these are recurring household actions. Users must recall different disclosure patterns across screens, and the primary action may be hidden while a lower-value action remains visible.

**Fix:** Keep destructive or occasional tools collapsed, but expose one primary action per surface: Add grocery, Add inventory item, and Edit or Mark cooked. Use the same label and placement pattern in English and Spanish.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill`

### [P2] Focus visibility and touch targets are uneven

**Evidence:** The global focus ring is gold at `styles.css:54-61`, which has weak contrast against the light surfaces; `.text-button` is only 38px high at `styles.css:1761-1769`, below the documented 44-48px target.

**Why it matters:** Keyboard users may lose the active control, and mobile users may hit small secondary actions inconsistently. The issue is uneven because some contexts override the base button size.

**Fix:** Use a darker, higher-contrast focus ring with a paired offset or surface change, and raise all actionable text buttons to at least 44px effective height while preserving compact visual weight.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable harden`

## Persona Red Flags

- **Caretaker in a grocery aisle:** Week requires horizontal scanning, then vertical movement to edit; inventory actions are nested; Add recipe is not in persistent navigation.
- **Low-technical-comfort family member:** Manage, List tools, and More actions conceal important actions. The Quick guide helps, but dismissing it removes the main explanation for where actions live.
- **Spanish-dominant household member:** The shell is translated, but recipe content may remain pending and disable grocery/cooking actions during network backfill.

## Minor Observations

- The Today title emphasizes dinner even though the broader product manages food, groceries, inventory, and recipes.
- Banner treatments vary enough by view that the app can feel like several related sections rather than one shared system.
- `DESIGN.md` still describes five navigation destinations while `index.html` currently exposes four; either the document or implementation should be made authoritative.
- The added 96px mobile bottom clearance is a good defensive fix, but it should be rechecked with the keyboard open and with the final inventory control visible.

## Questions to Consider

- Should Add become a fifth destination, or should the product deliberately keep four tabs and make Add a persistent primary action?
- Should Spanish users see source-language recipe content immediately while translation is pending, with a clear “translation in progress” state?
- Is the horizontal Week strip a valued gesture, or should the default mobile view prioritize a compact at-a-glance week?
