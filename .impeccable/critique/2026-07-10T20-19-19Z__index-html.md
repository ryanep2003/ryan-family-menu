**LLM assessment:** Borderline pass with moderate AI-slop risk. Today feels authored through real food imagery and concrete household shortcuts. Week, Groceries, Recipes, and Add regress to a familiar cream background, Inter, small uppercase labels, repeated bordered cards, and uniformly weighted controls. The result is coherent but only Today feels distinctive.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs --json index.html` returned exit 0 with zero findings. This is not a contradiction: the major problems are runtime density, composition, and repeated visual cadence rather than static-markup rule violations.

**Visual overlays:** No reliable user-visible overlay exists. Codex Browser evaluate is read-only, so mutable script injection was unsupported. Browser measurement was used as the fallback signal.

## Overall Impression

The app now behaves like a trustworthy household tool, but its visual confidence drops sharply after Today. The biggest opportunity is to carry the Shared Kitchen Table idea across all five views through compact food recognition, stronger contextual headers, and less card-and-control repetition.

## What's Working

- Today has a clear visual thesis with real food imagery, household shortcuts, and an asymmetric composition.
- English and Spanish fit at 390px without horizontal overflow, and focus/contrast fundamentals are strong.
- Tomato, sage, gold, and pantry neutrals retain disciplined semantic roles.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Recipe discovery is dominated by oversized images

**Why it matters:** The live Recipes view measured 8,199px in mobile English and 8,723px in Spanish. Twenty-two equally weighted cards make recognition slow.

**Fix:** Use compact 4:3 thumbnails, stronger title-first hierarchy, restrained metadata, and reserve full source-page imagery for detail.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable bolder Recipes`

### [P1] Empty Today is visually misleading

**Why it matters:** A meatball photograph remains behind repeated “No meal set yet” copy with a disabled action.

**Fix:** Use the planned main recipe image dynamically; when empty, use a neutral kitchen treatment and a direct Plan tonight action.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable clarify Today`

### [P1] Week still presents planning at too many scales

**Why it matters:** Seven summaries, a focused editor, and a 42-cell calendar remain on one page. “No meal set yet” repeats heavily.

**Fix:** Make the date strip compact and visually primary, reduce empty repetition, and subordinate the month overview.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable distill Week`

### [P2] Add exposes implementation structure

**Why it matters:** Three ingestion methods and nine fields form a long undifferentiated page.

**Fix:** Lead with intent, group fields into clear stages, and progressively disclose optional family metadata.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable shape Add recipe`

### [P2] Visual identity disappears outside Today

**Why it matters:** Repeated white cards and uppercase labels make the useful family tool feel templated.

**Fix:** Introduce one signature kitchen-table composition across views, with contextual color fields, compact food thumbnails, and varied but systematic section cadence.

**Suggested command:** `$impeccable bolder entire app`

## Persona Red Flags

**Busy parent cooking one-handed:** Empty Today has no next step, Week still scrolls too far for one dinner decision, and inventory actions are dense.

**Spanish-speaking caretaker:** Layout fit is strong, but weekday strings still omit accents in visible Spanish copy.

**Household power user:** At Home measured 6,194px and 190 controls on mobile; 170 controls are genuinely below 44px. Recipe and inventory scanning remain slower than necessary.
