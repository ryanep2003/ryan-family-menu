## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Medium AI-slop risk, not a failure. The household-specific handoff workflow is distinctive, but the parchment background, paper grain, editorial serif, tracked all-caps labels, irregular rounded cards, sage banner, and tomato accents resemble a saturated “warm recipe app” pattern. The repeating texture at `styles.css:55` is the clearest decorative excess for a task-focused product.

**Detector:** 79 advisory findings, all in `styles.css`: 48 font-size, 12 color, and 19 radius findings. `index.html` and `schedule-ui.js` were clean. Most are detector/design-document mismatches rather than confirmed defects: intentional metadata sizes, documented responsive heading sizes, semantic tonal colors, shadow colors already described in DESIGN.md, and asymmetric component radii. No detector finding was P0/P1.

**Browser evidence:** Browser control was available, but the local app could not be served. Impeccable's server ended at `ERR_SOCKET_BAD_PORT`; a fallback Python server was blocked by `PermissionError: Operation not permitted`, and localhost returned `ERR_CONNECTION_REFUSED`. No overlay was injected and no runtime desktop/mobile claims were made.

## Overall Impression

The Week view has the right core structure: seven summaries plus one focused editor. The biggest opportunity is to make planning feel like a quick household decision rather than a large form, while making save state and bilingual mobile navigation trustworthy.

## What's Working

- The seven-day summary plus one selected-day editor is sensible progressive disclosure, and selection moves focus to the editor.
- Family handoff choices support real leftovers, snack, and flexible-meal coordination.
- English and Spanish are structural, including translated labels and localized date formatting.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Week edits have no in-context save or recovery status.**  
Why it matters: `schedule-ui.js:99` saves on every change, but the shared status is inside Today. A caretaker editing Week cannot tell whether a change synced or conflicted.  
Fix: add a Week-local status near the header/editor with saved locally, syncing, synced, conflict, and retry states.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable harden`

**[P1] Spanish mobile week navigation is too narrow and below the touch-target intent.**  
Why it matters: three equal columns cannot safely fit “Semana anterior”, “Esta semana”, and “Semana siguiente” at phone widths. The controls are 40px at `styles.css:1129`, below the documented 44–48px target.  
Fix: use icon-plus-label previous/next controls, stack or wrap them safely, and give the current-week action a full-width row.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable adapt`

**[P1] Empty-day planning asks for too many decisions at once.**  
Why it matters: a simple dinner exposes main, side, salad, notes, and three handoff choices immediately. On mobile, the editor becomes a long form.  
Fix: make “Choose dinner” the first decision; reveal sides, salad, notes, and handoff progressively after a main meal exists.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable distill`

**[P2] The disclosed calendar loses useful information on mobile.**  
Why it matters: mobile hides meal summaries and source labels, leaving dates and dots. It is accessible to screen readers but visually weak for low-vision and hurried users.  
Fix: retain a compact meal label for the current week or pair the calendar with an agenda list, and clarify whether it is a date picker or a second planning surface.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable shape`

**[P2] The visual system is more styled than the planning task requires.**  
Why it matters: grain texture, asymmetrical radii, tracked labels, and the dark editorial banner compete with practical planning and reinforce a generic recipe-notebook aesthetic.  
Fix: remove the repeating texture, reduce decorative label treatment, and let family content and handoff copy carry personality.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable quieter`

## Persona Red Flags

- **Alex, power user:** Must open each day and fill individual fields; there is no copy-day, bulk-fill, keyboard shortcut, or fast weekly planning path.
- **Jordan, first-timer:** “Open calendar” and “Plan beyond this week” do not explain recurring weekly plans versus one-day overrides. The guide is not contextual to Week.
- **Sam, accessibility-dependent user:** Narrow Spanish navigation, 40px controls, dot-only calendar status, and Week-hidden sync feedback create avoidable barriers.

## Minor Observations

- The selected-day editor focus ring is strong and useful.
- “Clear week” correctly uses confirmation.
- Long recipe names remain available in accessible button labels even when visually clamped.
- The global update notice adds another sticky status layer above the Week experience.

## Questions to Consider

- Is Week primarily for comparing dinners, or editing one day? The current surface tries to do both.
- Should choosing a main dish feel like a complete success, with optional enrichment afterward?
- Can the interface's personality come from family content and handoff language instead of paper texture and recipe-book styling?
